Why Choose Prestigeride

What Separates a Car Service From a Chauffeur Service

Seven details we hold to a higher standard than the rest of the industry.

01

Licensed, Vetted Chauffeurs

Every chauffeur is licensed under applicable state and municipal for-hire regulations (including TLC licensing in NYC), background-checked, and defensive-driving certified. Training covers more than the route — it covers the quiet professionalism executive and diplomatic travel demands.

02

Live Flight & Route Tracking

Every airport transfer is linked to your flight number. Chauffeurs monitor arrival and delay data in real time and adjust pickup automatically, with 60 minutes of complimentary wait time built in.

03

A Fleet Held to One Standard

Every vehicle is under five years old, detailed before each reservation, and matched to the trip — Business Sedan, Luxury SUV, Executive Limousine, or Executive Sprinter for groups.

04

Fixed, Transparent Pricing

Your fare is confirmed at booking and doesn't move for time of day, demand, or traffic. Tolls and gratuity are itemized separately and shown up front — no surge, no surprise line items.

05

Advanced Booking Technology

Reserve online in under a minute, receive your chauffeur's name, photo, and vehicle details ahead of pickup, and track your car live from confirmation to arrival.

06

Corporate Transportation Accounts

Centralized monthly billing, traveler profiles, and a dedicated account manager for teams and executive assistants managing recurring ground travel.

07

24/7 Concierge Dispatch

A real person answers — for the 11pm itinerary change, the early-morning pickup, or the flight that lands three hours late.

Our Story

Founded on a Single Complaint

Prestigeride began in 2013 after our founder, a frequent business traveler, missed a closing meeting because a "luxury" car service sent a driver without directions and no way to reach dispatch. We built the company we wished had existed that morning: fewer cars, better vetted, and a concierge team that actually picks up.

Twelve years later, that standard hasn't moved. We've simply gotten better at holding it — across more cities, more fleets, and more of the moments that can't afford to go wrong.
